Fish N' Chips

Fish and chips is a beloved British dish that has captured hearts worldwide. The perfect combination of crispy, golden-brown batter and flaky, tender fish, served alongside fluffy chips, is a true culinary delight. This classic dish is easy to make at home and perfect for a weeknight dinner or a fun weekend treat.

Whether you prefer cod, haddock, or another firm white fish, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating a restaurant-quality meal in your own kitchen. So grab your apron, fire up your fryer, and get ready to indulge in a taste of British tradition.

Ingredients :

  • Firm white fish fillets (cod, haddock, or pollock)
  • Flour
  • Cornstarch
  • Baking powder
  • Black pepper
  • Cold sparkling water
  • Cold beer
  • Russet potatoes
  • Lemon wedges
  • Malt vinegar
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt

Steps :

  1. Season fish fillets with salt and pepper
  2. Dredge in flour, then dip in batter made from flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, pepper, beer, and sparkling water.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Carefully fry the battered fish until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Peel and cut potatoes into chips.
  6. Soak in cold water to remove excess starch.
  7. Dry the chips thoroughly with paper towels.
  8. Fry the chips in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.
  9. Drain the fish and chips on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Serve immediately with malt vinegar, lemon wedges, and tartar sauce (optional)
